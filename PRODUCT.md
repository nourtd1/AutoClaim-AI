# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Insurance operations teams and adjusters who process claims under time pressure. They use this tool continuously throughout their workday — monitoring pipelines, reviewing AI decisions, escalating edge cases. Context: professional, high-stakes, often under cognitive load from volume and urgency.

## Product Purpose

AutoClaim AI automates insurance claims intake, extraction, validation, and routing using Claude AI + UiPath Maestro orchestration. It exists to reduce manual review burden by 80%+, surface only the claims that genuinely need human judgment, and give ops teams real-time visibility into pipeline health. Success looks like a team confidently trusting the AI's decisions and spending their time on hard cases, not busywork.

## Brand Personality

Intelligent · Fluide · Moderne — a professional ops console that makes the team feel in control of something powerful. Confidence without arrogance. Precision without coldness.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS white: white cards, gray bg, indigo buttons — corporate template feel
- Bootstrap/MUI admin dashboards: tables-first, blue corporate, no identity
- Fintech overload: too many numbers, too many colors, visually exhausting
- AI hype landing pages: gradient text everywhere, purple glow with no purpose, mesh for mesh's sake

## Design Principles

1. **Cockpit clarity** — every piece of information has a purpose; nothing decorates for decoration's sake. The AI's confidence should be visible, not buried.
2. **State is the message** — color, animation, and typography exist to communicate claim state, actor type, and urgency. Never for aesthetics alone.
3. **Dark as context** — the dark theme isn't aesthetic choice for its own sake; it reduces eye strain for long ops sessions and makes status indicators pop against a neutral field.
4. **Tool disappears into task** — the UI should feel like a cockpit instrument panel: immediately readable, consistent, no surprises. Operators should build muscle memory.
5. **Earned trust** — every automated decision is visible in the audit trail. The system shows its work.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. All status indicators use color + shape (never color alone). Motion respects `prefers-reduced-motion`. Font sizes appropriate for dashboard density at standard DPI.
